Pressure Washing FAQs

What is the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing uses higher water pressure to remove dirt, grime, and buildup from durable surfaces like concrete, pavers, and patios. Soft washing uses lower pressure combined with specialized cleaning solutions to safely clean siding, roofs, and other delicate surfaces without causing damage.

How often should a house be washed?

Most homes in the Virginia Beach and Hampton Roads area benefit from professional house washing about once a year. Coastal humidity, salt air, and pollen can cause algae, mildew, and buildup to form on siding more quickly than in other regions.

Is roof cleaning safe for shingles?

Yes. Professional roof cleaning uses a soft washing method that removes algae, moss, and staining without using high pressure that could damage shingles. This process helps extend the life of your roof and improve curb appeal.

How often should commercial kitchen exhaust hoods be cleaned?

The NFPA 96 fire code requires commercial kitchen exhaust hood systems to be cleaned regularly. High-volume restaurants may require monthly or quarterly cleaning, while lower-volume kitchens may require semi-annual service. Regular hood cleaning reduces fire risk and keeps your kitchen compliant with safety regulations.
