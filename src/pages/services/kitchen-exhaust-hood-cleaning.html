Our Commercial Hood Cleaning Process

1. Pre-Inspection & System Evaluation

We inspect your entire exhaust system including the hood canopy, ductwork, and rooftop exhaust fan to assess grease buildup and determine cleaning frequency.

2. Protective Setup

We protect surrounding equipment, cooking surfaces, and floors before beginning service to keep your kitchen clean and operational.

3. Degreasing & Pressure Washing

Using commercial-grade degreasers and hot water pressure washing equipment, we remove heavy grease buildup from the hood, ductwork, filters, and exhaust fan.

4. Exhaust Fan & Duct Cleaning

We clean the entire vertical duct system and rooftop fan assembly to ensure full-system NFPA 96 compliance.

5. Final Inspection & Documentation

After cleaning, we perform a final inspection and provide documentation for your records and fire inspection requirements.

Certificate of Performance & Compliance Labeling

Upon completion, we apply a Certificate of Performance label in accordance with NFPA 96 requirements. This label documents the date of service, next scheduled cleaning, and technician verification for fire marshal and insurance inspections.

  • NFPA 96 compliant labeling
  • Last cleaned and next cleaning clearly marked
  • Supports fire marshal inspections
  • Provides documentation for insurance audits

Why Professional Hood Cleaning Is Critical for Restaurants

Grease buildup inside commercial kitchen exhaust systems is one of the leading causes of restaurant fires. When grease accumulates inside ductwork and exhaust fans, it becomes highly flammable and can ignite quickly under high cooking temperatures.

Regular commercial hood cleaning reduces fire risk, improves ventilation efficiency, and helps your business remain compliant with NFPA 96 standards and local fire codes. Failure to maintain proper cleaning schedules can result in failed inspections, insurance issues, and potential liability exposure.

  • Reduces grease fire risk
  • Improves air quality and ventilation
  • Helps pass health and fire inspections
  • Protects staff, customers, and property
  • Supports insurance compliance requirements
